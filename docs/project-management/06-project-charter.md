# Project Charter

## Project Name

Dealna - Local Deals Marketplace MVP for MENA

## Project Purpose

Dealna is a portfolio MVP for a local deals marketplace in the MENA region. The project demonstrates how a business idea can be translated into structured IT project documentation, delivery phases, technical requirements, risk planning, and measurable outcomes.

## Business Problem

Local businesses often promote offers through Instagram, WhatsApp, and informal channels. Customers do not have one reliable place to discover verified local deals, and merchants lack a simple way to publish offers and validate redemptions securely.

## Project Objective

Deliver an MVP where:

- Merchants can create business profiles and submit deals.
- Admins can approve businesses and deals.
- Customers can browse and claim active deals.
- Customers receive unique voucher codes and QR codes.
- Merchants can redeem vouchers exactly once.

## MVP Scope

### In Scope

- Customer registration and login.
- Merchant registration and login.
- Admin login.
- Role-based access control.
- Business profile creation.
- Business approval and rejection.
- Deal creation and submission.
- Deal approval and rejection.
- Public deal browsing.
- City and category filtering.
- Voucher claiming.
- QR code generation.
- Manual voucher code redemption.
- QR voucher redemption.
- Basic merchant analytics.
- Basic admin metrics.
- Audit log for sensitive actions.

### Out of Scope

- Native mobile apps.
- Paid voucher checkout.
- Payment provider integration.
- Merchant subscriptions.
- Advanced fraud scoring.
- Automated business verification.
- Multi-language UI.
- Advanced recommendation engine.
- Production-scale customer support tooling.

## Success Criteria

The project is successful when:

- The full end-to-end redemption flow works.
- A voucher cannot be redeemed more than once.
- Merchant and admin roles have clear permissions.
- Business and deal approval workflows are implemented.
- The app can be deployed and demoed.
- Documentation is clear enough for developer handoff and job application review.

## Assumptions

- The MVP will initially target one city.
- The first five categories are restaurants and cafes, beauty and grooming, fitness and wellness, entertainment and experiences, and hotels or staycations.
- The product will use a web-first approach.
- Manual admin review is acceptable for MVP.
- The first version will not process payments.

## Constraints

- Limited development capacity.
- Portfolio timeline.
- Need to control feature scope.
- Regulatory caution in medical, financial, tobacco, alcohol, and prescription categories.
- MVP should remain affordable to host.
