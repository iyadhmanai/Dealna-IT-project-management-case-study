# Stakeholder Analysis

## Purpose

This document identifies the main stakeholders for the Dealna MVP, their interests, expectations, influence, and communication needs.

## Stakeholder Map

| Stakeholder | Role in Project | Interest | Influence | Needs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product Owner | Defines product direction and MVP scope | High | High | Clear roadmap, priorities, risks, delivery status |
| Project Manager | Plans and coordinates delivery | High | High | Scope control, timeline, estimates, issue tracking |
| Developer | Builds the application | High | Medium | Clear requirements, acceptance criteria, technical decisions |
| Customer | Browses and claims deals | High | Medium | Useful deals, easy claiming, trusted vouchers |
| Merchant | Publishes and redeems deals | High | High | Simple onboarding, secure redemption, performance insights |
| Admin | Reviews businesses and deals | High | High | Approval queues, metrics, auditability |
| Business Owner or Sponsor | Evaluates business viability | High | High | Cost, risks, launch plan, measurable outcomes |
| Recruiter or Hiring Manager | Reviews portfolio value | Medium | High | Evidence of PM thinking, structure, clarity, practical delivery |

## Stakeholder Expectations

### Customers

Customers expect:

- Active and relevant local deals.
- Clear deal terms.
- Simple claim process.
- Voucher status visibility.
- Confidence that vouchers will work at the business.

### Merchants

Merchants expect:

- Easy business onboarding.
- Ability to create and manage deals.
- Fast in-store voucher redemption.
- Protection against duplicate voucher use.
- Basic analytics to evaluate deal performance.

### Admins

Admins expect:

- Clear pending review queues.
- Ability to approve or reject businesses and deals.
- Visibility into marketplace metrics.
- Audit logs for sensitive actions.

### Development Team

Developers expect:

- Stable MVP scope.
- Prioritized requirements.
- Clear acceptance criteria.
- Technical architecture decisions.
- Testable business rules.

### Hiring Managers

Hiring managers reviewing this as a portfolio project expect:

- Structured thinking.
- Practical IT project management artifacts.
- Awareness of scope, risk, cost, and quality.
- Ability to connect business goals to implementation work.

## Engagement Strategy

| Stakeholder | Engagement Approach | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product Owner | Roadmap and scope review | Weekly |
| Developer | Sprint planning, backlog refinement, blockers | 2-3 times per week |
| Admin Representative | Workflow review and acceptance testing | Per milestone |
| Merchant Representative | Usability feedback on onboarding and redemption | Per milestone |
| Customer Representative | Feedback on browsing and claiming flow | Per milestone |
| Sponsor | Status reporting and risk review | Weekly or biweekly |

## Stakeholder Risks

| Risk | Affected Stakeholder | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer does not trust deal quality | Customer | Admin approval and clear deal terms |
| Merchant finds redemption too slow | Merchant | Mobile-first redemption page and fast validation |
| Admin workload becomes high | Admin | Simple review queues and clear statuses |
| Sponsor requests too many features | Project team | Use MoSCoW prioritization and change control |
| Hiring manager sees project as too technical only | Applicant | Include PM artifacts and business rationale |
